## Workplace Accommodation Rights by Country

### United States — Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA, 1990)

**Who is covered:** Employees and job applicants with physical or mental impairments that substantially limit major life activities, at employers with 15+ employees.

**Key rights:**
- Employer MUST provide "reasonable accommodations" unless it causes "undue hardship"
- You do NOT have to disclose your disability during the interview
- Employer cannot ask about disability before making a job offer
- Accommodations include: modified schedule, remote work, assistive technology, job restructuring, accessible parking, sign language interpreter

**How to request:** Put your request in writing to HR. Say: "I am requesting a reasonable accommodation under the ADA." Provide medical documentation if asked. The employer must engage in an "interactive process" with you.

**If denied:** File a charge with the EEOC (eeoc.gov) within 180 days. Free to file. You can also contact your state's disability rights organization.

**Resource:** Job Accommodation Network (JAN) — askjan.org — Free expert consultation on any accommodation question.

### United Kingdom — Equality Act 2010

**Who is covered:** Anyone with a physical or mental impairment that has a "substantial and long-term" (12+ months) adverse effect on daily activities.

**Key rights:**
- Employer must make "reasonable adjustments"
- Includes physical changes, policy changes, auxiliary aids
- Access to Work (ATW) government program pays for accommodations (up to ~$66,000/year)
- Cannot be treated less favorably because of disability

**How to request:** Contact your employer in writing. Apply for Access to Work at gov.uk/access-to-work — the government pays, not your employer.

**If denied:** Contact ACAS for free advice (acas.org.uk), then Employment Tribunal if needed.

### Canada — Accessible Canada Act (ACA) + Provincial Laws

**Who is covered:** All federally regulated employees. Provincial laws (like Ontario's AODA) cover provincial employers.

**Key rights:**
- Duty to accommodate to the point of "undue hardship"
- AODA (Ontario) requires accessibility plans, training, and standards for all employers with 20+ employees
- Other provinces have similar legislation
- Federal workplaces must be barrier-free by 2040

**Key provincial laws:**
| Province | Law |
|----------|-----|
| Ontario | Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (AODA) |
| Manitoba | Accessibility for Manitobans Act |
| British Columbia | Accessible British Columbia Act |
| Nova Scotia | Accessibility Act |

**How to request:** Submit written request to employer. If federal, contact Canadian Human Rights Commission (chrc-ccdp.gc.ca). If provincial, contact your provincial human rights commission.

### European Union — Employment Equality Directive (2000/78/EC) + European Accessibility Act (2025)

**Who is covered:** All EU member states must implement the directive. Coverage varies by country but generally covers all employers.

**Key rights by country:**
| Country | Key Law | Notable Features |
|---------|---------|-----------------|
| Germany | SGB IX | 5% disability quota for employers with 20+ staff; non-compliance = levy |
| France | Law of Feb 11, 2005 | 6% quota for employers with 20+ staff; AGEFIPH fund for accommodations |
| Netherlands | Participation Act | Wage subsidies for employers hiring disabled workers |
| Spain | General Disability Act (LGD) | 2% quota for employers with 50+ staff |
| Italy | Law 68/1999 | Mandatory hiring quotas based on company size |
| Sweden | Discrimination Act | Broad protection, strong enforcement |

**European Accessibility Act (2025):** Requires products and services (including computers, smartphones, e-commerce, banking) to be accessible across all EU member states.

### Australia — Disability Discrimination Act 1992

**Key rights:**
- Covers employment, education, access to premises, goods/services
- Employer must make "reasonable adjustments"
- JobAccess (jobaccess.gov.au) — Free workplace assessment + funding for modifications
- Employment Assistance Fund pays for workplace modifications and assistive tech

### India — Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act 2016

**Key rights:**
- 4% reservation in government jobs for persons with disabilities
- Covers 21 categories of disabilities (expanded from 7)
- Incentives for private employers hiring disabled workers
- National Fund for Persons with Disabilities provides grants

## Tips for Disclosing Disability (Your Choice, Your Terms)

Disclosing a disability is always **your personal decision**. There is no legal requirement to disclose during the application process in most countries.

### When Disclosure May Help
- You need accommodations for the interview (sign language interpreter, extra time, virtual option)
- The employer has a known disability hiring program (SAP, Microsoft, etc.)
- Your disability is visible and you want to address it proactively
- The role is specifically for disabled candidates (government quota positions)

### When to Be Cautious
- During initial screening (bias is highest at the resume stage)
- When you don't need any accommodations
- At companies with no visible disability inclusion efforts

### How to Disclose (If You Choose To)
Keep it brief, professional, and focused on solutions:

> "I have [condition] which means I work best with [specific accommodation]. With this in place, I deliver excellent results, as shown by [specific achievement]. I wanted to mention this so we can set things up for success from day one."

You are explaining what you need, not apologizing for who you are.

## Final Message

Disability is not inability. The barriers you face are most often created by environments, systems, and attitudes — not by your body or mind.

The world of work is changing in your favor: remote work is normalized, assistive technology is advancing rapidly, legal protections are expanding, and companies are realizing that disability inclusion is not charity — it is competitive advantage. Disabled workers bring unique perspectives, problem-solving abilities, and resilience that no training program can teach.

You deserve a career that values what you bring. This guide is here to help you find it.
