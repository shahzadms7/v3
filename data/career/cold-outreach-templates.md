## SECTION 1: LINKEDIN CONNECTION REQUESTS (5 Versions)

*LinkedIn limit: 300 characters for connection note. Every word counts.*

### Version 1: Applying to Their Company

> Hi [FIRST NAME], I recently applied for the [JOB TITLE] role at [COMPANY]. I've been following [COMPANY]'s work on [SPECIFIC INITIATIVE/PRODUCT] and would love to connect. Thank you for your time!

### Version 2: Shared Background (Alumni, Industry, City)

> Hi [FIRST NAME], fellow [UNIVERSITY NAME] grad here — [YOUR GRADUATION YEAR]. I'm now in [YOUR FIELD] at [YOUR COMPANY OR "exploring new opportunities"]. Would love to connect with another [SCHOOL] alum in [THEIR FIELD]. Hope you're well!

### Version 3: Admired Their Content

> Hi [FIRST NAME], your recent post on [SPECIFIC TOPIC] really resonated — especially your point about [SPECIFIC INSIGHT]. I work in [YOUR FIELD] and would love to connect and learn from your perspective.

### Version 4: Recruiter at Target Company

> Hi [FIRST NAME], I'm a [YOUR TITLE/FIELD] professional with [X] years in [INDUSTRY]. I'm very interested in opportunities at [COMPANY], specifically in [DEPARTMENT/AREA]. Would love to connect — I'd welcome any guidance.

### Version 5: Referred by Someone

> Hi [FIRST NAME], [MUTUAL CONTACT NAME] suggested I reach out. I'm exploring [FIELD/ROLE TYPE] opportunities and they mentioned you'd be a wonderful person to connect with. I'd appreciate any time you could spare.

## SECTION 3: FOLLOW-UP AFTER NO RESPONSE (3 Sequences)

### Sequence 1: After Application (No Response in 7 Days)

**Subject:** Following up — [JOB TITLE] application | [YOUR NAME]

> Dear [NAME],
>
> I wanted to follow up on my application for the [JOB TITLE] position submitted on [DATE]. I remain very enthusiastic about this opportunity.
>
> To briefly reiterate my fit: I bring [X years] in [FIELD] with a track record of [TOP ACHIEVEMENT IN ONE SENTENCE].
>
> I understand you're likely reviewing many candidates, and I appreciate your time. If there's any additional information I can provide, please don't hesitate to ask.
>
> Thank you,
> [YOUR NAME]

**If still no response after 7 more days:**

> Dear [NAME],
>
> I'm writing one final time regarding the [JOB TITLE] role. I respect your time and won't follow up again after this.
>
> If the position has been filled or my profile isn't the right fit, I completely understand. If the process is still ongoing, I'd love to be considered — I'm confident I can [ONE SPECIFIC VALUE YOU'D BRING].
>
> Either way, thank you for your consideration. I wish [COMPANY] continued success.
>
> Best regards,
> [YOUR NAME]

### Sequence 2: After Cold Email (No Response in 5 Days)

**Subject:** Re: [ORIGINAL SUBJECT LINE]

> Hi [NAME],
>
> I know your inbox is busy — just bumping this to the top in case it got buried. Would love to connect for a brief 10-minute conversation about [TOPIC/ROLE].
>
> If the timing isn't right, no worries at all. Let me know either way and I'll adjust accordingly.
>
> Thanks,
> [YOUR NAME]

**If still no response after 7 more days (final follow-up):**

> Hi [NAME],
>
> Last note from me on this — I don't want to overstay my welcome in your inbox.
>
> If [COMPANY] ever needs someone with [YOUR KEY SKILL], I'd be grateful to be considered. My LinkedIn is below if you'd like to stay loosely connected.
>
> Wishing you a great [week/quarter],
> [YOUR NAME]
> [LINKEDIN URL]

## SECTION 4: THANK YOU EMAIL AFTER INTERVIEW (3 Versions)

>

>
> Our conversation reinforced my excitement about this opportunity. The chance to [SPECIFIC CONTRIBUTION — e.g., "build the analytics dashboard from scratch" or "lead the customer onboarding redesign"] aligns perfectly with what I'm looking for in my next role.
>
> I also wanted to address [SOMETHING FROM THE INTERVIEW — a question you could have answered better, or an additional thought]:
> [1-2 sentences adding value or clarifying your answer.]
>
> Please don't hesitate to reach out if you need any additional information. I look forward to hearing about next steps.
>
> Best regards,
> [YOUR FULL NAME]
> [YOUR PHONE NUMBER]

## QUICK REFERENCE: OUTREACH DOS AND DON'TS

| DO | DON'T |
|----|-------|
| Personalize every message with specific details | Send identical templates to 100 people |
| Keep LinkedIn requests under 300 characters | Write essays in connection notes |
| Reference their work, posts, or company news | Make it entirely about yourself |
| Offer specific value or a small ask (15 min) | Ask for a job in the first message |
| Follow up once or twice, then stop | Send 5+ follow-ups |
| Send thank-you emails within 24 hours | Wait a week to follow up after interview |
| Use professional subject lines with your name | Use "URGENT" or clickbait subjects |
| Proofread every message twice | Send messages with typos or wrong names |
| Respect time zones (send during business hours) | Send messages at 2 AM their time |
| Be genuine — people detect fakeness instantly | Use manipulative or overly salesy language |
