# Resume Best Practices 2026 — Top 1% Standard
**What separates top candidates from the 75% rejected by ATS**

## ATS Optimization Rules
1. Use standard section headings: Experience, Education, Skills, Certifications
2. No tables, columns, graphics, or headers/footers (ATS can't parse them)
3. File format: PDF preferred, .docx acceptable, never .pages or image
4. Font: 10-12pt, standard fonts (Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman)
5. Keywords: Match EXACT phrases from job description (not synonyms)

## The 6.2-Second Scan Test
Recruiters spend 6.2 seconds on first scan. They look at:
1. Current job title + company (top left)
2. Years of experience (quick math)
3. Education + certifications (scan bottom)
4. Keywords that match the job (everywhere)
5. Numbers and metrics (dollar signs, percentages)
If these 5 things don't match in 6.2 seconds → REJECTED.

## STAR Method for Every Bullet
- Situation: Set the context (1 line)
- Task: What was your responsibility
- Action: Specific steps YOU took (not the team)
- Result: Quantified outcome (%, $, time saved, people impacted)

BAD: "Managed team projects and improved processes"
GOOD: "Led 12-person team to deliver $2.3M platform migration 3 weeks early, reducing infrastructure costs by 34%"

## Scoring Weights (How Top Recruiters Actually Score)
| Category | Weight | What They Check |
|----------|--------|-----------------|
| Relevant Experience | 30% | Years, titles, industry match |
| Achievement Density | 25% | Numbers per bullet, results focus |
| Skills Match | 20% | Hard skills matching JD keywords |
| Education + Certs | 15% | Degree, relevant certifications |
| Format + Clarity | 10% | Clean, scannable, no errors |

## Red Flags That Get Instant Rejection
- Employment gaps > 6 months unexplained
- Job hopping (3+ jobs in 2 years without explanation)
- Generic objective statement instead of professional summary
- No quantified achievements (just job duties listed)
- Typos or grammatical errors (instant credibility loss)
- Resume longer than 2 pages (unless 15+ years senior)
- Missing contact info or unprofessional email
- Buzzwords without evidence ("results-oriented leader")

## 2026 Resume Trends
- AI-assisted resume writing is standard (but AI-ONLY resumes are detectable)
- Interactive/hyperlinked PDFs increasing (+35% response rate with portfolio links)
- Skills-based hiring growing (some employers dropping degree requirements)
- "Career Co-pilots" emerging: AI + human expertise replacing single-doc services
- Video resume supplements gaining traction for creative/sales roles
- Personal branding sections ("My Values") trending for startup culture fit
