# Enterprise Data Retention Policy v3.0
**Effective: January 1, 2026 | Classification: Internal | Owner: Chief Compliance Officer**

## 1. Purpose
Mandatory data retention and disposal requirements for all company data assets.

## 2. Financial Records
- Tax records: 7 years from filing date
- Audit reports: 7 years from completion
- Invoices and receipts: 5 years from transaction
- Payroll records: 7 years after termination
- Bank statements: 5 years

## 3. Employee Records
- Active employee files: Duration of employment + 7 years
- Recruitment records (not hired): 2 years from decision
- Performance reviews: 5 years after termination
- Training records: Duration of employment + 3 years

## 4. Customer Data
- Active customer records: Duration of relationship + 5 years
- EU customer data (GDPR): Maximum 3 years post-relationship unless legal obligation
- Customer complaints: 5 years from resolution
- Contract records: 10 years from expiration

## 5. IT and Security
- Access logs: 2 years
- Security incident reports: 7 years
- System backups: 90 days rolling
- Email archives: 5 years

## 6. Data Disposal
- Digital: NIST 800-88 compliant cryptographic erasure
- Physical: Cross-cut shredding DIN 66399 Level P-4 minimum
- All disposal requires Data Owner + Compliance Officer approval
- Documented in Disposal Log with date, method, witness

## 7. Legal Holds
- ALL disposal suspended when litigation anticipated
- Legal department issues hold notice within 24 hours
- Violation of legal hold is a terminable offense

## 8. GDPR Requirements
- Right-to-erasure requests fulfilled within 30 days
- Data transfers outside EU require Standard Contractual Clauses
- DPIA required for new processing activities involving EU personal data
